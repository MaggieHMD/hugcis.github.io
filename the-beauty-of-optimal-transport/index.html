The beauty of Optimal Transport

2018-12-07

A simple problem ?

Let's start at the beggining: what is Optimal transport ?

It all begins with Gaspard Monge, reading his mémoire in front of eminent people (including Condorcet) from the French Académie Royale des Sciences in 1776. Monge's problem could be explained as follows:

Given two excavations \(D_1\) and \(D_2\), a cost of moving a unit of mass of earth from any point of \(D_1\) to any point of \(D_2\),

What is the cheapest way to move the mass from \(D_1\) to \(D_2\) ?

Turns out, the answer is not at all simple. Monge didn't solve it in his mémoire, although he did lay the foundations of modern optimal transport theory.

But let's move to the problem.

In it's simplest form, it can be viewed as an assignment problem: among all configurations, which one is the best ?
